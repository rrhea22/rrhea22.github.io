Shaping an AI-Powered Tutoring Platform as Sole Researcher

Company Step Up Tutoring (EdTech Startup)
Role Sole UX Researcher
Timeline Oct 2025 – May 2026
User Interviews Usability Testing Session Replay Contextual Observation Surveys A/B Preference Testing Behavioral Analytics User Segmentation PostHog
Case study image

Overview

Step Up Tutoring built an AI-powered tool to help volunteer tutors generate and solve math problems during live sessions with 2nd–6th grade students. As the sole UX researcher, I led two phases of mixed-methods research that drove 5 shipped product changes within 3 weeks and enabled confident scaling to a pilot of 40 volunteer tutors. Roughly 70% of recommendations were adopted across both phases.

The Challenge

The product team was ready to scale their AI tutoring tool — but without knowing whether tutors could actually use it under live session pressure, scaling carried real risk. Research had to match the pace of development across two distinct product stages.

Research Approach

Research was structured in two phases to match the product's development trajectory.

1. Rapid Iteration: Oct – Dec 2025

Goal: Validate usability before scaling

Methods

Live observation + interviews (3), usability testing (4 sessions), stimulated recall with AI session summaries, A/B preference survey


2. Pilot Testing at Scale: Jan – May 2026

Goal: Monitor adoption and surface usability issues across 40 tutors

Methods

Session replay review, user interviews (5), usage check-in survey, bi-weekly tutor panel sessions (30 users), lightweight feedback collection, user segmentation

What Shipped — Within 3 Weeks

Feature Research Finding Product Change
Tool Architecture Discoverability was a major pain point "AI Whisper" renamed to "Tutor Sidekick," restructured as a sidebar combined with Session Briefing
Interaction Flow Recurring confusion around minimizing and accessing the tool Sidebar format made access intuitive, eliminating the confusion pattern
UI Language Function names were unclear and non-actionable Three Sidekick function names revised for clarity; explanation wording simplified
Tutor Control Problem sets locked to 3 regardless of student need Tutors can now adjust problem set size and difficulty on the fly
AI Feedback Model Session summaries missed non-verbal cues Model refined to recognize non-verbal interaction cues for more accurate summaries

Partnered closely with product leadership to deliver insights at the pace of development — Phase 1 findings translated into shipped product changes within 3 weeks.

✦ Insight Confidence Framework

Developed a framework categorizing findings by how many methods corroborated them (High / Medium / Low) — so stakeholders could make prioritization decisions based on finding reliability, not just researcher judgment.

Key Learnings

01 Scope Each Study to One Decision

Limiting studies to 1–2 focused questions is what let findings land fast enough to influence the build.


02 Behavioral Data and Self-Report Tell Different Stories

Tutors said the tool was "fine" — session replay showed them clicking the same button 3–4 times and skipping features entirely.


03 Adapt Methods to Scale

What worked at small N wasn't feasible at 40 tutors — adapting methods to scale was necessary for research to stay credible.

This research directly improved usability and helped the product team confidently scale the tool to a broader pilot group of 40 volunteer tutors.
