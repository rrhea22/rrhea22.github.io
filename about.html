Rhea (Rong) Ren, Ph.D.

UX Researcher  ·  Mixed-Methods  ·  AI & Product Research

I came to UX research through an unexpected path: a Ph.D. in Applied Linguistics from Arizona State University, where I spent years studying how people learn language — the cognitive patterns, the social contexts, the moments where understanding breaks down. Studying how humans make sense of complex systems turned out to be excellent training for research.

Over the past four-plus years, I've brought that mindset to products across AI EdTech, educational nonprofits, civic tech, and agency work embedded at Google. My sweet spot is being the researcher who gets brought in early — before the wireframes, before the sprint planning — because I believe research is most valuable when it shapes the problem definition, not just the final design.

I'm a mixed-methods researcher at heart: comfortable running a 12-person interview study and comfortable building a Qualtrics survey with custom branching logic. I use behavioral analytics (PostHog is my current tool of choice) alongside qualitative work, because the what and the why belong together. I also speak Italian, which gives me an outsized appreciation for how much language shapes user experience.

These days, AI is woven into how I work. I use Claude, ChatGPT, Dovetail AI, and Granola as research collaborators — for synthesis, for pattern exploration, for pressure-testing my own interpretations. I've also done direct evaluative work on AI outputs: assessing LLM accuracy, building issue taxonomies for AI-generated content, and understanding where models fail real users. I bring that practitioner's perspective to any team building with AI.
